What is KOSEN?

KOSEN (National Institute of Technology) is a unique Japanese higher education system offering a five-year integrated program for training highly practical, hands-on engineers, starting right after junior high school.

These colleges focus on intense technical education, incorporating significant lab work, practical training, and research to produce skilled professionals for industry, often seen as the fastest route to becoming an advanced engineer.

Department

Computer Engineering

    The Computer Engineering program focuses on the design, development, application, and problem-solving of computer systems and operations in all aspects. It also aligns with global trends in areas such as AI, IoT, and Network & Cyber Security.

Mechatronics Engineering

    The Mechatronics Engineering program integrates knowledge from mechanics, electronics, and computer science, with a focus on robotics. It aims to produce and develop smart technologies for industrial applications.

Electrical Engineering

    The Electrical and Electronics Engineering program combines electrical and electronic disciplines, applying and developing expertise in these two fundamental areas of engineering. It also includes learning about the use of renewable and clean energy for advancing global technology.
